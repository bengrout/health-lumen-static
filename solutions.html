Solutions

Our solutions are applied in both the public sector (e.g. governments, health charities) and private sector (e.g. pharmaceutical companies, investors in emerging biotech companies).

Our solutions are based on a leading-edge micro-simulation model, developed over the past 12 years, which has the capability to model trends in risk factors and disease, and quantify the impact of policy options and interventions.

The micro-simulation model simulates virtual populations of up to 100 million people, and projects dynamic trends in risk factors (e.g. obesity, smoking, alcohol, air pollution) into the future.

Different scenarios, or interventions can be modelled against a baseline or 'no change' scenario to quantify the long-term impact of that intervention e.g. a sweet drinks tax, a smoking cessation service, reaching air pollution targets.

Some examples of how we assist decision making:

Generating evidence for policy change

We generate evidence to leverage and support the case for policy change. We do this by creating virtual populations to quantify the impact of health policy measures on chronic diseases and related costs, now and into the future.

Epidemiological analysis and data science

Population health is underpinned by epidemiology. We provide epidemiological expertise in risk factor and disease progression from systematic literature reviews, data science and innovative analytics, to complex epidemiological and economic modelling.

Evaluation of randomised trials into the future

We evaluate the long-term epidemiological and economic impact of community based or clinical interventions to determine their effectiveness and cost-effectiveness over the next 5, 10, 20 years into the future, without the cost and ethical implications of a long trial.

Investment decisions for emerging health technologies

Supporting decision making by understanding the long-term impact and return on investment of new apps, medical devices, or tools is important. We do this by first simulating incidence and prevalence of diseases into the future, and then modelling the impact of these new devices/drugs/biotechnologies to quantify the year on year return on investment, lives saved, and diseases avoided.

Track record

Our micro-simulation model was developed as part of the UK Health Forum's program in public health policy research and advocacy. HealthLumen acquired the modelling platform from UKHF in June 2019.
