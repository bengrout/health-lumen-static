Team

Health Lumen has an experienced and multi-disciplinary team of software engineers, population health and policy research experts, and mathematicians.

Simon Lande, PhD
Co-Founder and CEO

Simon Lande is co-founder and CEO of HealthLumen. Prior to this, Simon was the CEO and founder of ActiveStandards, a pioneering digital governance and compliance software service, designed to help multinational corporations ensure quality and accuracy across their global web presence. ActiveStandards became the established market leader, resulting in a US private equity acquisition in November 2015.

Simon holds holds a BSc from Leeds University, and a PhD in Pharmacology from the Royal Free Hospital School of Medicine (University of London), which he followed with post-doctoral research on the neuromuscular junction, in the Department of Biochemistry at Imperial College, London.

Simon has a longstanding involvement with the National Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Children (NSPCC), including a year spent on the Executive Board (2017-2018) conducting a strategic review of income generation.

Laura Webber, PhD
Co-Founder and COO

Laura Webber is co-founder and COO of HealthLumen. Prior to this, Laura was Director of Public Health Modelling at the UK Health Forum, and led a multi-disciplinary team of epidemiologists, mathematicians, analysts, and computer programmers, co-ordinating global, European and national projects.

Projects included the European Commission funded project 'EConDA' (Economics of Chronic Diseases), the Trust For America's Health project 'F as in Fat', modelling obesity across each US state as well as various projects for Public Health England, Cancer Research UK, World Health Organization, and the World Bank.

Laura holds an MA (Hons) from Cambridge University and a PhD in childhood obesity from University College London (Cancer Research UK studentship). She is honorary Assistant Professor at the London School of Hygiene and Tropical Medicine within the Health Protection Research Unit, and an occasional advisor to the World Health Organization and World Bank.

She is lead author and co-author on a number of peer-reviewed publications and major reports. Laura was nominated for the Young Investigator of the year at Europrevent, Amsterdam (2015) and recently won the Open University Future Leader Award (2018), where she is studying for an MBA.
